Who am I and how can you help?


Hi, my name is Renato, I am a former student of the Waldorf School, and I work in an emergency call center, listening to the voices and stories of those who need help in their most vulnerable moments. This work teaches me every day about empathy and the power of communication as the key to breaking down barriers and building a more inclusive world.

During college, I took a Libras course, where I learned how important it is to be able to communicate and express oneself effectively, between people who are oral and those who are not, even if only in a basic way, with people who use sign language in their daily lives.

At that moment, I realized the challenge that these people face every day, whether in seeking assistance from basic education, health and safety services, or in the simplest everyday situations. This reality motivated me to want to be part of the solution.

Today, while I am studying for my master's degree in Production Systems at the Paula Souza Center, I am developing a real-time sign language translation application.

With this tool, I want to reach out to deaf people who will use the app on their cell phones to express their needs and be understood clearly, while computer system operators will have access to instant translation and will be able to communicate more welcomingly with deaf people.

It is a bridge between two worlds. Today, in Brazil, around 22.4% of people with hearing impairments have some basic knowledge of Libras, but communication is still a barrier that must be faced every day.

Imagine the impact of making these people's lives easier every day, their daily interactions. Today, there are more than 400 million people who use sign language in the world.
